HUH?, 4 November 2004
Author:
tenpercent from Midwest - USA
I gave this 3 stars out of a possible 10.The film goes along never explaining anything really.We never find out for certain whether Dr. Adams is being visited by
spirits, by Satan himself, or if he having a psychotic breakdown or
what. I guess its a case of - you take your pick - whichever scenario
you feel like going with.The story concerns Dr. Ty Adams, a handsome young man, who feels mental
patients can be cured by therapy, that medication is not needed.He comes to Sedah Hospital to prove his theory, and to have it all
filmed by cameras set up throughout the institute as well as a camera
crew being present.The main patients Dr. Adam's deals with are Cheryl a young woman who
was badly abused as a child and who tends to go into defensive rages
combined with depressive withdrawal, and a man who claims he is Satan.Why Satan voluntarily admits himself to the mental hospital is never
explained. Nothing in this film ever really goes anywhere. No point is made, and a
complete explanation is never given.
